The Discovery of Subatomic Particles

The revolutionary realization that atoms themselves have internal structure

1897: The Electron

J.J. Thomson discovers the electron through cathode ray experiments, revealing tiny negatively charged particles within atoms

1909: The Nucleus

Ernest Rutherfords gold foil experiment reveals a dense, positively charged nucleus at the atoms center

1919-1932: Protons and Neutrons

Rutherford identifies protons and James Chadwick discovers neutrons, completing the nuclear picture

Each discovery built upon the last, revealing ever-smaller building blocks of matter.
