Quantum Mechanics Foundations

Quantum mechanics is a fundamental theory in physics that provides a description of the physical properties of nature at the scale of atoms and subatomic particles. It is the foundation of all quantum physics including quantum chemistry, quantum field theory, quantum technology, and quantum information science.

At its core, it describes a world where probability takes precedence over certainty, and energy is quantized into discrete packets.

Wave-Particle Duality

Every particle or quantum entity may be described as either a particle or a wave. This is a central concept of quantum mechanics.

Uncertainty Principle

It is impossible to know both the position and momentum of a particle with absolute precision.

Entanglement

Particles can become correlated such that the state of one instantly influences the other.

Detailed Biological Classification

Biological classification is the scientific method in which biologists group and categorize organisms by biological type, such as genus or species. Modern biological classification has its root in the work of Carl Linnaeus, who grouped species according to shared physical characteristics. These groupings have since been revised to improve consistency with the Darwinian principle of common descent and genetic similarity.

The hierarchy of biological classification involves eight major taxonomic ranks, each narrower than the one before it. This system allows for global collaboration and precise identification of every living thing discovered on Earth.

1

Domain

The highest taxonomic rank. There are three domains: Archaea, Bacteria, and Eukarya.

2

Kingdom

Animalia, Plantae, Fungi, Protista, Archaea, and Bacteria are the primary kingdoms.

3

Phylum

Groups organisms with a similar body plan, like Chordata or Arthropoda.

4

Class

A taxonomic rank below phylum. For example, Mammalia or Insecta.

5

Order

For example, Primates or Lepidoptera. It narrows down the organism type further.

6

Family

For example, Hominidae or Felidae. Represents closely related genuses.
