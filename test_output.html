ENIAC and Vacuum Tubes

Vacuum tubes functioned as electronic switches, allowing ENIAC to perform calculations faster than mechanical relays.

ENIAC contained about 17,468 vacuum tubes, contributing to its size.

Key Takeaways:
  • ENIAC was the first electronic general-purpose computer built in 1946.
  • Vacuum tubes were crucial components in ENIAC.
  • ENIAC's vacuum tubes generated a significant amount of heat.
